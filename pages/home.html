A little about myself

I have a combined experience of about eleven years that includes instructional designing, eLearning development and graphic designing. I have been working in the eLearning industry for the past five-and-a-half years.

Award: One of my project has received Honorable Mention IELA 2020. The project was a blended learning soft-skill course: Guided Self-Coaching from Schulich School of Business.

I was born and brought up in India. I immigrated to Canada a couple of years back.

I started my career as a graphic designer as I have always enjoyed doing artwork. I have worked in corporate branding, media and publishing houses and created logos, advertisements, promotional ads, posters, giant hoardings and book cover designs. At times I had also provided my clients with contents as demanded. Later, I went for content writing and worked as a freelance content writer for educational institutions.

The shift to the eLearning world happened when one of the popular and prosperous NGO in India offered me to be a part of their growing eLearning team, where I had brought in some fresh development ideas for the online curriculum. I worked to develop eLearning contents, training materials and design instructions for various courses. I worked 3 years in India as an instructional designer and a lead developer for core eLearning companies. Many of my clients were from North America. Presently, I am a Learning Experience Designer at a Canada based eLearning Company. I leverage my extensive knowledge in multimedia tools to support the course development in creating intuitive learning experiences for my target audiences. I have recently completed Instructional Design Certificate program from OntarioLearn.

When I am not working, I like to spend my time painting and photographing.
