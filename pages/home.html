A little about myself

I have a combined experience of a total of ten years that includes instructional designing, eLearning development and graphic designing. I have been working in the eLearning industry for the past four-and-a-half years.

I am born and brought up in India. I immigrated to Canada last year as a Permanent Resident.

My career had started as a graphic designer as I have always enjoyed doing artwork. I worked for corporate branding, media and publishing houses to create logos, advertisement artwork, promotional ads, book cover designs for authors, posters and giant hoardings. If the situation demanded, I also provided my clients with content, which I took seriously later and freelanced as a content writer for some educational institutes.

The shift to the eLearning world happened when one of the popular and prosperous NGO in India offered me to be a part of their growing eLearning team, where I had brought in some fresh development ideas for the online curriculum. I worked to develop eLearning contents, training materials and designing instructions for various courses. I worked 3 years in India as an instructional designer and a lead developer for core eLearning companies with many clients being from North America. Presently, I am a Learning Experience Designer at a Canada based eLearning Company. I leverage my extensive knowledge in multimedia tools to support the course development in creating intuitive learning experiences for my target audiences. In the pursuit of further knowledge, I am continuing part-time education on the Instructional Design program and Adult Learning from George Brown College in Toronto.

When I am not working, I like to spend my time painting and photographing.
