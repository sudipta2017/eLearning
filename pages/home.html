A little about myself!

I am a Learning Experience Designer based in Toronto, Canada. I have a combined experience of total ten years that includes instructional designing, learning and development and graphic designing. I have been working in eLearning industry for the past four-and-a-half years.

I am born and brought up in India. I immigrated to Canada last year as a Permanent Resident.

I started my career as a graphic designer as I have always enjoyed artwork. I worked for media and publishing houses, to create book cover designs for authors, logos, advertisement artwork, promotional for companies, posters and giant hoardings. If the situation demanded, I also provided my clients with content, which I took seriously later and freelanced as a content writer for some educational institutes.

The shift to the elearning world happened when one of the popular and prosperous NGO in India appreciated my work in media and content development and offered me to be a part of their eLearning team. I accepted the opportunity and continued to enhance my skills in development of online curriculum. I worked to develop eLearning contents, training materials and designing instructions for various courses. I worked 3 years in India as an Instructional Designer and Lead Developer for core eLearning companies with many clients being from North America. Presently, I am a Learning Experience Designer at a Canada based eLearning Company. I leverage my extensive knowledge in multimedia tools to support the course development and create intuitive learning experiences for my target audiences. In the pursuit of further knowledge, I am continuing part-time education on Instructional Design program and Adult Learning from George Brown College in Toronto.

When I am not working, I like to spend my time painting and photographing.
